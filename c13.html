Result For Your Skin







Gentle Cleansing:

Start your routine with a gentle, oil-balancing cleanser specifically formulated for oily skin. Look for a cleanser that contains brightening ingredients like niacinamide or kojic acid to help fade hyperpigmentation while removing excess oil and impurities. Cleanse your face twice daily, morning and night, to keep pores clear and prevent breakouts.

Exfoliating Treatment:

Incorporate an exfoliating treatment into your routine to help fade hyperpigmentation and promote skin renewal. Choose a chemical exfoliant containing alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs) like glycolic acid or beta hydroxy acids (BHAs) like salicylic acid to gently slough away dead skin cells, unclog pores, and improve skin texture. Start with a lower concentration and gradually increase frequency as tolerated

Brightness Serum:

Apply a brightening serum containing ingredients like vitamin C, alpha arbutin, or licorice extract to target hyperpigmentation and even out skin tone. These ingredients help inhibit melanin production, fade existing dark spots, and brighten the complexion. Apply the serum twice daily, morning and night, after cleansing and before moisturizing.

Hydrating Moisturizer:

Choose a lightweight, oil-free moisturizer specifically designed for oily skin that also addresses hyperpigmentation concerns. Look for a non-comedogenic formula that contains hydrating ingredients like hyaluronic acid and niacinamide to replenish moisture without clogging pores. Apply the moisturizer evenly to your face and neck morning and night after cleansing and serum application.

Broad-Spectrum Sunscreen:

Finish your routine by applying a broad-spectrum sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher to protect your skin from further UV damage and prevent dark spots from worsening. Opt for a lightweight, non-greasy formula that won't exacerbate oiliness or cause breakouts. Reapply sunscreen every 2 hours, especially when outdoors or during prolonged sun exposure.
